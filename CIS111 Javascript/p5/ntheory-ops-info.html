Number Theory Operators

Deficient

A deficient number is a number n for which the sum of divisors Ïƒ(n) less than 2n, or, equivalently, the sum of proper divisors (or aliquot sum) s(n) less than n.

Perfect

A positive integer that is equal to the sum of its proper divisors.

Abundent

An abundent number is a number for which the sum of its proper divisors is greater than the number itself.

Prime

This button will display whether the number is prime or not. A prime number is divisiable by itself and 1.
